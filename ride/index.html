The frame
AI adoption is about people, not prompts.
Most AI sessions are either engaging or useful, not both. The fun part shouldn’t be a detour from the useful part. It is how the useful part begins. Disruption Joe
When people are genuinely engaged, the result is better insight, stronger buy-in, and more momentum for what comes next. From the Activations Playbook

How a session moves.

01

Arcs

The shape of a session.
Example arc · ARC-007
Opportunity to Prioritized Backlog
Explore → Judge → Decide
A field of ideas becomes a ranked shortlist the team owns. Each arc has a recognizable shape you can feel as the room moves through it.
02

Plays

Structured exercises.
Example play · PLY-003
Use-Case Prioritization
The room scores opportunities against value, effort, and fit, then pulls a clear shortlist. Quick wins separate from deeper bets, and people leave owning the priorities.
03

Moves

Reusable AI interactions.
Example move · MOV-020
Persona Lens Review
“Look at this draft as our most skeptical customer. What lands? What does not? Where does it lose them?”
A move is a small habit you can take with you. Once you have felt it in the room, you can run it on your own afterward.
04

Outputs

Artifacts you keep.
Example output

Captured12 candidate use cases Top tier3 ranked: value × effort × fit OwnerYou. Yours to act on this week.
Every play produces something tangible. Notes, frameworks, named decisions. You leave with the work itself.

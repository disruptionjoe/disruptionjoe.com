Everyone is telling you to use AI. Almost no one is showing you *where it actually fits your work*.

The internet is full of AI advice that sounds useful in the abstract — "use ChatGPT to write emails faster" or "automate your workflow with AI" — but collapses the moment you sit down and try to apply it. The advice is generic because it was never grounded in a specific person's actual day.

## The real question isn't "how do I use AI?"

It's "what am I doing every day that AI could change?"

That's a different question entirely. It requires someone to look at your work — your actual tasks, your decision patterns, where you spend time, where you get stuck — and then identify the spots where AI is genuinely worth the effort.

Most of those spots aren't the ones you'd guess. They're not about writing emails faster. They're about:

- Recognizing patterns in data you're already looking at
- Drafting the first version of something you've been procrastinating on
- Turning a messy conversation into a clear action plan
- Stress-testing a decision before you commit to it

These are specific, contextual, and personal. They don't fit neatly into a listicle.

## What actually works

The people I've seen get the most out of AI are the ones who stop treating it as a general productivity tool and start treating it as a thinking partner for specific problems they already have.

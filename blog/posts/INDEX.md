# Writing Samples

A curated selection of writing that demonstrates how I think. These are not blog posts. They're working documents that reflect how I approach complex problems: structurally, clearly, and with an eye toward systems that actually work.

---

## Included in this collection

### [Accelerating the Arrival of the Paradigm of Collaboration](accelerating-collaboration.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Big-picture systems thinking, synthesizing historical patterns into a forward-looking thesis, and a personal stance grounded in structural analysis rather than ideology.

**Summary:** Why coordination systems built on coercion are failing, what new tools (open networks, AI, incentive design) make possible, and what a paradigm of collaboration would look like in practice.

---

### [From Governance Theater to Operational Meritocracy](governance-to-meritocracy.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Practical systems design, diagnosing structural problems in organizations, and a hypothesis-driven approach to building better coordination mechanisms.

**Summary:** Why DAOs struggle with execution when deciding and doing are separated, and a concrete framework for reconnecting authority to contribution.

---

### [The Output > Outcome > Impact Value Creation Pipeline](output-outcome-impact.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Analytical clarity, creating useful frameworks from complex problems, and practical thinking about accountability and measurement in decentralized systems.

**Summary:** How ecosystem funding creates value across three timescales (outputs, outcomes, impact), with parallels drawn across DAOs, cities, and nations.

---

### [The Next Trillion Dollar Opportunity: Cost Centers, Revenue Centers, and the Missing Coordination Layer](dao-proposal-process-broken.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Economic reasoning applied to organizational design, and the ability to see decentralized networks as a new structural form rather than just a technology.

**Summary:** How megacorporations coordinate through cost/revenue centers, why startups trade scale for speed, and how a coordination layer could deliver the advantages of both.

---

### [DAOs Are Not Just Onchain Businesses](daos-not-just-onchain-businesses.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Conceptual precision about what makes decentralized organizations genuinely different from corporate structures.

**Summary:** Why treating DAOs as onchain corporations misses the point, and what it means to design organizations capable of permissionless participation at global scale.

---

### [What DAOs Can Learn From Packet Routing](daos-packet-routing.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Cross-domain thinking, applying network architecture principles to organizational design.

**Summary:** How the internet's modular, edge-intelligent routing model offers a better design template for DAOs than monolithic voting.

---

### [Decentralized Autonomous Organizing as an RPG](decentralized-organizing-as-rpg.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Creative framing of participation mechanics, applying game design to organizational behavior.

**Summary:** Why the best decentralized networks will feel more like RPGs than companies, designing contribution progression and trust-building before governance.

---

### [The Future of DAO Funding: Continuous, Decentralized, and Efficient](future-of-dao-funding.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Mechanism design thinking, applying proven protocol incentive patterns to organizational governance.

**Summary:** Replacing batch voting-based funding with continuous streaming models using reward epochs, threshold governance, and issuance curves.

---

### [How Woke Should Your AI Be? Exploring Truth, Constraints, and Community Governance](how-woke-should-your-ai-be.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Applying governance frameworks to AI systems, treating constraint-setting as a legitimacy problem.

**Summary:** The tension between AI base models seeking truth and societal constraints, and governance models for who decides AI behavior parameters.

---

### [We MUST Privatize Social Safety Nets](privatize-social-safety-nets.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Economic trend analysis, arguing structural inevitability rather than ideological preference.

**Summary:** Why government institutions cannot match market speed as AI and robotics compress labor costs, and how private networked systems will inevitably replace government safety nets.

---

### [Protocol Labs and the Rise of Network Incentive Systems](protocol-labs-network-incentives.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Historical analogy and structural analysis of what drives innovation at scale.

**Summary:** Parallels between Bell Labs' golden era and the future of innovation competition, arguing that network structure (not individual speed or capital) is the decisive factor.

---

### [Voting is Dead](voting-is-dead.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Separating mechanisms from goals, rethinking collective decision-making from first principles.

**Summary:** Why voting is an obsolete batch process designed for information scarcity, and how continuous sensing and voice mechanisms produce legitimacy through participation rather than ballots.

---

### [What Every DAO Is Getting Wrong Today (Part 1/4)](what-every-dao-gets-wrong.md)
**What it demonstrates:** Diagnostic clarity, identifying where DAO designs import corporate/bureaucratic patterns instead of leveraging decentralization's actual advantages.

**Summary:** Three critical mistakes: optimizing for revenue over autonomy, failing to define product/impact linkage, and emphasizing voting over sense-making.
